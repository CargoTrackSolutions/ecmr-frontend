Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

Technology and the online environment bring benefits to all of us, but also challenges — one of which is protecting the personal data of our customers. At Cargo Track Solutions S.R.L., this has been a firm commitment to our customers from the very beginning.

As of 25 May 2018, Cargo Track Solutions S.R.L. has adopted EU Regulation 2016/679, known as the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), which is in force across all Member States of the European Union.

We assure you that Cargo Track will continue to process customers' personal data only for well-defined purposes, such as creating personalised offers and conducting market research on Cargo Track products and services.

2. What is personal data and how do we collect it?

Personal data is any information by which a customer can be directly or indirectly identified. Cargo Track obtains this data as follows:

Directly from the customer

Cargo Track collects personal data directly from the customer — typically information provided when signing or during the course of a contract:

  • Full name and date of birth
  • Postal address and email address
  • Phone number and other contact details
  • Transaction details (products purchased, prices, payment methods)
  • Traffic details and the area from which the customer uses Cargo Track services

Cargo Track also receives any information or content actively created and provided by the customer (photos, clips, etc.).

In addition, to be able to offer the best products and services, Cargo Track may sometimes request information about the customer's experience while using Cargo Track products and services. This may include comments, suggestions, or improvement recommendations.

Automatically

In certain situations, Cargo Track may also collect customer data through automated means. For example, personal data may be collected via cookies, web or phone beacons when the customer interacts with Cargo Track services. Data collected in this way may include: IP address, browser type, operating system, mobile device identification number, geographic location, redirect URLs, and information about actions taken or interactions with Cargo Track.

3. Who is responsible for personal data in the case of group offers?

In the case of group offers or products and services serving multiple users, the account holder is responsible for the data they provide. The options expressed by the account holder regarding personal data will apply to all users in the created group (unless another user has indicated their own option in advance). In case of discrepancies between user and account holder options, the account holder's option prevails.

4. Why do we process personal data?

Cargo Track will process customers' personal data only to ensure the best possible relationship during the course of the contract. Processing may be based on the customer's consent, the contractual relationship, or a legal obligation.

When Cargo Track requests the customer's consent for processing, three options are available: "Yes", "No", or "Later". Selecting the last option entitles Cargo Track to send subsequent consent requests.

Only with the customer's consent, Cargo Track will process personal data to provide information about:

  • Cargo Track products and services, offers, promotions, or special events
  • Products and services of Cargo Track affiliates or business partners

As necessary for the provision of products and services, Cargo Track will process personal data to:

  • Fulfil customer requests/orders/enquiries and process payments for Cargo Track products and services
  • Verify customer eligibility for certain offers and products

Based on legitimate interest that does not infringe the customer's rights, Cargo Track will process personal data to:

  • Personalise the online Cargo Track experience and request feedback on the customer's experience
  • Create and maintain customer accounts, including the administration of loyalty or reward programmes
  • Ensure necessary protection, identification, and prevention of debts, abuse, and complaints
  • Assess potential fraud or late payment risks, including checks against dedicated databases
  • Assess customer solvency and commercial risk prior to entering into a contract
  • Improve Cargo Track products and services by evaluating user access and usage patterns
  • Build customer profiles to subsequently identify fraud cases

Cargo Track may record telephone calls with its representatives for the purpose of identifying customer needs and improving services. The customer is informed of this at the time of requesting a call with a Cargo Track Customer Service operator and may decline the call.

5. What are customers' rights regarding the processing of personal data?

Customers have specific rights with respect to data protection, and Cargo Track maintains an environment that facilitates the exercise of those rights (right of access, rectification, objection, erasure, data portability, and to lodge a complaint).

The customer has the right to:

  • Access their personal data, modify it, restrict processing, withdraw consent, object to processing, or request its erasure (right to be forgotten)
  • Access their personal data or request that Cargo Track transfer it to another company (right to data portability)
  • Deactivate certain types of data collection or use, including the use of certain cookies and similar technologies
  • Lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority — in Romania this is the National Supervisory Authority for Personal Data Processing (ANSPDCP)
  • Request that Cargo Track correct any inaccuracies in their personal data

The right to opt out may be exercised by: following the opt-out instructions in relevant marketing communications, modifying preferences in the online account settings, or by submitting a written request to Cargo Track.

The customer will continue to receive administrative communications from Cargo Track (order confirmations, account activity notifications) even if they opt out of marketing communications.

6. How does GDPR apply to children?

Cargo Track does not knowingly request or collect personal data from children under the age of 16 when providing specific services (unless the explicit prior consent of a parent or guardian has been obtained), or under the age of 14 in all other situations. Where personal data of children is collected, it is processed with care and only with specific safeguards in place.

7. What does automated processing of personal data entail?

Any decision made solely on the basis of automated processing will either be previously consented to or be necessary for the conclusion of the contract. Cargo Track personalises communication with its customers using personal data related to customer history, previous choices, or product and service preferences.

The customer will not be subject to any decision based solely on automated processing of data that produces legal effects or significantly affects them, unless they have explicitly agreed to such processing, it is necessary for the conclusion or performance of a contract with Cargo Track, or Cargo Track is required by law to use personal data in this way.

In all cases, the customer may request an explanation of the logic behind the automated decision-making mechanism, and where applicable, may request human intervention in the decision-making process.

8. Can your personal data be processed by third parties?

As a general rule, Cargo Track will not share the customer's personal data with a third party that intends to use it for direct marketing purposes, unless the customer has consented to this. Cargo Track may however share customers' personal data with third parties, without their consent, in the following situations and with the following entities:

  • Affiliates: personal data may be shared with Cargo Track affiliated companies for the purposes of conducting current business activities
  • Service providers: Cargo Track may engage service providers, agents, or contractors to provide services on its behalf, all of whom are required to comply with applicable data protection laws and specific security requirements regarding the customer's personal data
  • Other telecommunications service providers: when reporting payment delays, where applicable
  • External consultants or partners: such as external lawyers, debt collection agencies, and call centres
  • Public authorities: Cargo Track may share customer data when required to do so by law or when it reasonably believes such disclosure is necessary for the proper conduct of legal proceedings or investigations

9. How do we protect your personal data?

Data security

To ensure the security of personal data, Cargo Track has implemented a range of security measures in line with industry standards. These do not cover personal data that the customer chooses to share in public online or offline spaces.

Data retention

Cargo Track will retain personal data for as long as necessary for the stated purpose, taking into account the need to respond to queries or resolve issues, to provide new or improved services, and to comply with applicable legal requirements.

When personal data is no longer required for the purpose for which it was collected and there is no legal obligation to retain it, Cargo Track will destroy or delete it in a secure manner.

Processing of personal data

We process your personal data exclusively within the provisions of applicable data protection law, including EU Regulation 679/2016 and Law no. 506/2004 on the processing of personal data and the protection of privacy in the electronic communications sector.

Our employees and authorised processors are obliged to comply with data protection provisions in accordance with applicable legal standards.

For individuals who have applied to a job posted by the company, the data submitted via the application form is stored in Cargo Track's secure database for a period of 3 years. To request earlier deletion of your data, please send a request by email to office@cargotrack.ro.

10. How do these regulations apply to legal entities?

For customers that are legal entities, Cargo Track will obtain the explicit consent of the account holder for the types of personal data processing for which the law requires such consent. The customer as a legal entity undertakes, before transferring personal data of users and contact persons to Cargo Track, to obtain their consent for the processing carried out by Cargo Track in the performance of the contract.

By transferring the personal data of users and contact persons to Cargo Track, the customer as a legal entity attests that the consent of the users and contact persons has been obtained for the processing of their personal data by Cargo Track as described above, and assumes full responsibility in this regard.
